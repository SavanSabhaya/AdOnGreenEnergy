1. Identifying Sources
❐ On-site generation: If your organization produces significant organic waste, consider setting up a biogas plant.
❐ Third-party suppliers: Partner with suppliers that process waste into biogas.
2. Supplier Assessment
❐ Evaluate suppliers based on sustainability practices, production capacity, and compliance with regulatory standards.
❐ Check for certifications like ISO 14001 or others relevant to environmental management.
3. Quality Standards
❐ Ensure the biogas meets specifications, especially if it’s intended for specific applications like electricity generation, vehicle fuel, or direct heating.
4. Logistics and Delivery
❐ Establish agreements on how biogas will be transported (pipeline, containers, etc.).
❐ Factor in storage requirements if on-demand usage is critical.
5. Regulatory Compliance:
❐ Adhere to environmental, safety, and emissions standards.
❐ Obtain necessary permits for procurement and utilization.
6. Economic Viability
❐ Compare costs of biogas against other energy sources.
❐ Explore incentives, subsidies, or carbon credits that might make biogas procurement more attractive.
7. Long-term Contracts
❐ Secure supply agreements to ensure consistent biogas availability.
❐ Negotiate terms like pricing, quantity, and quality assurances
8. Technology and Infrastructure
❐ Ensure your facility is equipped to utilize biogas (e.g., compatible boilers, generators, or fuel systems).
